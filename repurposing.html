CREATING AFFORDABLE HOUSING

Austin’s continued racial and economic segregation stems from the fact that the areas with the best public resources (school, parks, etc.) only contain expensive, single-family housing, or properties with high square footage values. Naturally, the best way to combat this would be to create affordable housing in these wealthier areas.

OFFICE SPACES

One way to create affordable housing in Austin is to use unused office spaces. Due to the coronavirus pandemic, many businesses have been shut down, and those who haven’t been have moved their operations online. This leaves a lot of empty office space.

Repurposing this empty office space for housing would be effective due to the fact that office spaces are abundant throughout Austin, meaning that affordable housing would be able to be created in all Austin communities, wealthy or poor.

PARKING LOTS

An alternative to using un-occupied offices would be to use empty parking lots. There are numerous parking lots in the Austin area that are either unoccupied, or too large given the amount of traffic they receive on a daily basis.

Using parking lots as a source of housing space would be great in that buildings would be able to be constructed relatively quickly, as there’s no existing structures to tear down. This source of affordable housing would be especially effective in Austin’s suburban areas, where parking lots are more common compared to in Austin’s metropolitan area.

WHAT'S NEXT?

If you see a parking lot or office space that would be great for repurposing, contact a local politician about it. Constructing affordable housing begins with changing the zoning laws that govern these parking lots and office spaces, and with enough support that change can certainly be made.
